College

I graduated from The University of North Carolina at Charlotte in May 2024. My time at UNCC was transformative. Not only did I gain a broad and deep education in computers and technology, but I also underwent a personal change. I began pursuing a degree in 2020, and lockdowns changed everything about that experience. I struggled during this time to maintain myself and to engage with my studies, however, each year I improved. I first got on the dean's list in the second semester of junior year and maintained that throughout my senior year, finishing my last semester with a 4.0 GPA for the semester. I have learned to take pride in my work, rather than reject it. More than anything else, this change in attitude has made me a more whole person.

As far as the relevant skills I gained, my degree in computer science covered all the major topics, and my concentration in AI brought me deep into the field of data science and programming. Combined with my pre-university education, I have experience with a wide variety of software and programming languages, including all industry-standard languages such as SQL, SAS, Python, C, and R, as well as libraries like NumPy and Pandas.

When I first came to Charlotte, I was more focused on software engineering. However, over the last few years, I have come to realize that data science and analytics are my true calling. As part of my concentration in AI, I got to work on machine learning algorithms and relational database systems. I was immersed in mathematics as well as technology, covering linear algebra, calculus, and statistics. All of these systems interacting seems to be the most useful place of development for society. We make many assumptions as humans without realizing how limited our experience is, and data science is one way we can dispel many harmful myths and reduce our negative impact on the world, both socially and physically.
